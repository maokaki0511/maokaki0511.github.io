About Me

Designing through structure, curiosity, and continuous creation.

I’m Kaki Mao, a UI/UX designer based in Tokyo. I design structured digital experiences for B2B SaaS, workflow systems, and real-world operations.

Who I Am

I care about how people understand information, make decisions, and move through complex tasks.

I’m especially interested in design problems that involve unclear information structures, complex workflows, and real-world operational contexts.

To me, design is not only about making interfaces look polished. It is about understanding where people get stuck, how tasks should be organized, and how a product can make a difficult process feel clearer and more reliable.

What Brought Me Here

My path into design has been shaped by curiosity, practice, and the need to keep moving forward.

01 / Choosing Design

Why I chose design

I didn’t choose design only because I liked beautiful visuals. What attracted me more was the process of understanding problems: why something feels confusing, where people hesitate, and how structure can make things easier to use.

02 / Learning by Doing

Internship and growth at SAP

During my internship at SAP, I worked on enterprise tools, internal workflow products, design system-related tasks, video design, and motion design. These projects taught me that design is rarely about a single screen.

03 / New Environment

Moving to Japan

After graduation, I moved to Japan and started working as a UI designer in Tokyo. Working in a new country has been challenging, but it has also made me more aware of how language, culture, and context shape communication and product experience.

04 / Current Direction

Growing toward product design

I’m now focusing on becoming a more product-oriented designer who can understand business context, clarify user problems, and create reliable experiences for complex systems.
