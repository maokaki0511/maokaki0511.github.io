Wallet

Creating a seamless event experience through digital check-in and engagement design.

  • B2B
  • UX & UI
  • Mobile Web

Overview

During my long-term internship at SAP, I was responsible for designing the web-based wallet check-in experience for SAP DCOM Shanghai, a large-scale corporate event with around 3,000 participants.

Challenge

How might we help organizers manage event participation and lottery eligibility while encouraging employees to actively attend sessions?

To increase employee engagement during DCOM Shanghai 2023, the organizer introduced a lottery mechanism: employees who actively participated in sessions and completed check-ins would become eligible for the prize draw.

However, this created a new management challenge. With thousands of participants and multiple sessions happening throughout the event, it became difficult to manually track attendance, participation progress, badge collection, and lottery eligibility.

Final Solution

A mobile-first digital wallet system that connected event check-ins, agenda information, badge collection, and lottery qualification into one seamless experience.

We designed a digital wallet experience for DCOM Shanghai 2023, enabling participants to check in with QR codes, view event schedules, collect badges through participation, and track their lottery eligibility.

Outcome

The product successfully supported a large-scale internal event and became a recognized case within the organization.

The Wallet system was launched for DCOM Shanghai 2023 and supported more than 2,600 participants throughout the event.

Discovery

Understanding the event journey from both participant and organizer perspectives.

At the beginning of the project, we analyzed the full event flow and identified several key user scenarios entering the venue, browsing the agenda, checking in to sessions, collecting activity badges, and confirming lottery eligibility.

Opportunity

Turning a functional check-in tool into an engaging event companion.

Instead of treating the product as a simple QR-code check-in system, we saw an opportunity to make it part of the event experience itself.

Ideate

Designing around clarity, motivation, and fast on-site decision making.

Based on the event scenarios, I explored how to organize the information architecture and user flow around participants' real-time needs.

UI Design

Creating a clear and event-friendly interface under a tight timeline.

As the only designer on the project, I was responsible for the interface design from structure to high-fidelity screens, while closely communicating with developers and stakeholders.

Takeaways

This project helped me practice designing for operational clarity under real event constraints, balancing participant motivation with organizer-side tracking needs.
